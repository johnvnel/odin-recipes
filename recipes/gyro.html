Description

Gyro is a popular Greek dish made of seasoned meat, typically lamb, beef, or chicken, that is cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The meat is traditionally seasoned with a blend of herbs and spices like oregano, garlic, and cumin. It is thinly sliced and served in a warm pita bread, often accompanied by fresh toppings like tomatoes, onions, and a creamy, tangy tzatziki sauce made of yogurt, cucumber, and garlic. Gyro is a flavorful and satisfying meal, commonly enjoyed as a quick lunch or dinner. Its combination of savory meat, fresh veggies, and rich sauce makes it a beloved street food worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs (680g) lamb, beef, or chicken (or a combination)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 4 pita breads or flatbreads
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, sliced thinly
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Tzatziki sauce (Greek yogurt, cucumber, garlic, lemon juice, and dill)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat:

    Slice the meat into thin strips. In a bowl, mix the garlic, oregano, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, salt, pepper, olive oil, lemon juice, and red pepper flakes (if using). Add the meat to the marinade, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Cook the meat:

    Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated meat for 5-7 minutes, or until browned and cooked through. If using a rotisserie (traditional method), layer the meat and cook it on a vertical spit.

  3. Assemble the gyro:

    Warm the pita bread, and add the cooked meat. Top with sliced tomatoes, onions, fresh parsley, and a generous dollop of tzatziki sauce.

  4. Serve:

    Roll up the pita around the fillings and serve immediately.
