Baked Alaska

Home

Description

Baked Alaska is a dramatic and indulgent dessert that combines cake, ice cream, and meringue. A layer of sponge cake or biscuit forms the base, topped with a scoop of ice cream. The entire creation is then covered with a thick layer of meringue and quickly baked in a hot oven, just enough to brown the meringue without melting the ice cream inside. The contrast between the warm, toasted meringue and the cold ice cream makes Baked Alaska a show-stopping dessert. It’s often served on special occasions, offering a delightful combination of textures and temperatures that make each bite a unique experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 box of sponge cake mix (or homemade sponge cake)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 cups vanilla ice cream (or any flavor you prefer)
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Prepare the cake:

    Preheat your oven according to the cake mix instructions. Prepare the sponge cake by following the directions on the box (or make a homemade version). Once baked, allow it to cool completely.

  2. Prepare the ice cream layer:

    Line a bowl (or a small dish) with plastic wrap. Scoop ice cream into the bowl and press it into the shape of a dome. Freeze for several hours or overnight until firm.

  3. Assemble the Baked Alaska:

    Cut the cooled cake into a circle slightly larger than the ice cream dome. Place the cake on a baking sheet. Unmold the ice cream onto the cake.

  4. Make the meringue:

    Beat egg whites and cream of tartar in a clean bowl until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar and vanilla extract, and continue beating until stiff peaks form.

  5. Cover with meringue:

    Spread the meringue over the ice cream and cake, making sure to seal the edges.

  6. Bake:

    Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Bake the Baked Alaska for 3-5 minutes, just enough to brown the meringue (do not let the ice cream melt).

  7. Serve:

    Slice immediately and serve!
